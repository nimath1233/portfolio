ðŸ“‹CV Structure & Essential Components

I learned that a professional CV must follow a clear, organized layout. The lecturer showed us how information should be easily accessible through bullet points, headings, and ample white space. I was advised to use professional fonts like Inter, Calibri, or Arial at 10-12pt sizes, with consistent margins throughout.

Essential Sections I Need to Include:

a. My Personal Information

I will list my full name, professional phone number, email, and location. I also make sure to link my LinkedIn profile and this portfolio website.

b. My Professional Summary

I learned to write a concise 3-4 sentence overview of my career goals, key skills, and unique value, specifically tailored to the target role.

c. My Work Experience

I need to list my relevant work experience in reverse chronological order. For each, I should include bulleted accomplishments using action verbs.

d. My Education

I will list my degrees, majors, university name, and graduation year in reverse chronological order, including coursework or honors where relevant.

e. My Skills Section

I learned to include a balanced mix of technical hard skills (like React, Java, and Git) and soft skills, categorized logically.

f. My Achievements & Awards

I want to showcase certifications, awards, scholarship details, and hackathon wins to demonstrate my proactive attitude to employers.

I learned that a cover letter is a formal one-page document sent alongside my CV. I realized its purpose is to introduce myself, explain my interest in the specific company, and highlight why I am the ideal fit for the job.

  • The Hook: I learned to address it to the hiring manager by name, opening with the role I am applying for.
  • Why Them?: The session showed me how to express genuine interest by mentioning the company's values, projects, or reputation.
  • Why You?: I should highlight 2-3 key accomplishments from my CV using specific, quantifiable examples to prove my skills.
  • The Close: I learned to state my desire for an interview, thank the reader, and include a polite sign-off.

I realized that my cover letter is not a repeat of my CV; it is a personal narrative that connects my credentials directly to the employer's specific needs.
