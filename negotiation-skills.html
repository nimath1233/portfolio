7 Key Negotiation Principles I Learned:

People Before Problems

I learned to separate the relationship from the issue, being soft on the people but firm on the merits.

Trust & Integrity

I realized honest communication builds trust, which makes it much easier to find solutions that help both sides.

Focus on Interests

The session showed me to look at what parties actually need (interests) rather than what they say they want (positions).

Create Options

I learned to brainstorm multiple possibilities that offer mutual gains before deciding on a final choice.

Objective Criteria

I will divide resources based on fair standards, logic, or market value, never by pressure.

Mind Power & Co-Ownership

I must stay calm, listen actively, and ensure the other party feels they helped shape the final agreement.

âš–ï¸Understanding & Identifying My BATNA

I learned that BATNA stands for Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. I realized it is my fallback position if a negotiation fails, and acts as my main source of bargaining power:

  • Bargaining Anchor: My BATNA tells me exactly when to walk away from a bad deal. If a proposal is worse than my BATNA, I should not accept it.
  • Preparation Steps: I learned that to identify my BATNA, I must list all actions I can take, select the best alternative, and strengthen it.
  • Examples: I realized that if I am negotiating a salary, having another job offer is my BATNA, giving me the power to negotiate confidently.

I learned that negotiation requires combining active listening, questioning, and rapport-building. I must select the right strategy for the situation:

Collaborative (Win-Win)

I learned that both parties can work together to expand the value (pie). I will prioritize this for team relationships and clients.

Compromising (Split Difference)

Finding a fast middle ground where both parties make equal concessions. I will use this when time is short.

Competitive (Win-Lose)

Focuses entirely on claiming value at the expense of the other side. I should avoid this to protect my relationships.

Strategic Empathy: I learned to practice strategic empathy, seeing the situation from the other party's perspective to help find a compromise.
