I learned that workplace skills can be divided into three main categories. Understanding these helps me build a well-rounded professional brand:

  • Technical/Hard Skills: My job-specific knowledge and expertise (e.g., coding in React, configuring databases).

ðŸªŸThe Johari Window Self-Assessment

I learned about the **Johari Window**, a cognitive model developed in 1955. We used it to enhance my self-awareness and understand what is known and unknown to myself and to others.

1. Open Area (Arena)

Known to myself and known to others. I learned that good communication and feedback help increase this area.

2. Blind Area (Blind Spot)

Known to others but unknown to myself. I realized I can reduce this area through active feedback from peers.

3. Hidden Area (Facade)

Known to myself but unknown to others. I learned I can reduce this area through appropriate self-disclosure.

4. Unknown Area

Unknown to myself and unknown to others, representing hidden talents or potential I haven't discovered yet.

Developing My Professional Skills:

  1. 1
    Seek Constructive Feedback: I will ask team members or instructors about my strengths and areas for improvement to reduce my blind spots.
  2. 2
    Practice Self-Disclosure: I want to share my relevant goals and ideas with my colleagues to build mutual trust and expand our Open Area.
  3. 3
    Commit to Lifelong Learning: I will continuously seek workshops and experiences to unlock capabilities in my Unknown Area.
