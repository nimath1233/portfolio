Interview Skills & Job Hunting

Mock interview and peer evaluation practice

💼What is a Job Interview & Best Strategy?

I learned that a Job Interview is a structured conversation where an employer evaluates my qualifications, compatibility, and communication, while also being my chance to evaluate the company.

The Best Interview Strategy

I learned to be prepared and honest, focusing on how I can solve the employer's problems rather than just what I want from them.

Role of Communication

I realized that how I say things is as important as what I say. I need to focus on clear articulation and structured answers.

📊The 7-38-55 Rule of Communication

The lecturer showed us Albert Mehrabian's 7-38-55 rule, which highlights the weight of non-verbal cues in face-to-face communication when emotions are involved:

7%

Spoken Words: I learned this is the literal content of my answers.

38%

Vocal/Tone: The pitch, volume, speed, and warmth of my voice.

55%

Body Language: My eye contact, smile, hand gestures, and posture.

Applying the Rule: I will practice sitting tall, maintaining eye contact, and speaking at a steady pace to project confidence.

⚔️Successful vs Unsuccessful Candidates

I learned that hiring teams notice behavioral differences instantly. I will keep these indicators in mind:

✅ Successful Candidate (How I should behave)

✔ I will listen actively and answer the exact question.
✔ I will use technical/industry terms correctly.
✔ I will project confidence and positive body language.
✔ I will ask smart, research-backed questions.

❌ Unsuccessful Candidate (What I must avoid)

✘ I must avoid giving vague, generic, or off-topic answers.
✘ I will avoid looking at the floor, fidgeting, or slouching.
✘ I should never speak negatively about past team members.
✘ I must stay humble and avoid claiming to know everything.

⭐The STAR Storytelling Method

The session showed me how to use the **STAR Method** to answer behavioral questions. I realized it will keep my stories structured, clear, and impactful:

S — Situation

I will describe the context and background of my project briefly, keeping it short and relevant.

T — Task

I need to explain the challenge or problem, and what my specific responsibility was in that situation.

A — Action

I must detail the exact steps I took, making sure to use "I" instead of "we" to explain my contribution and skills.

R — Result

I will highlight the outcome using numbers where possible, and share what I learned from the experience.

💡 Worked STAR Example:

Situation: In our 2nd-year database project, my team was struggling to connect our MERN front-end to the MySQL database because of configuration mismatches.
Task: As the team's backend developer, my goal was to resolve the database error within 24 hours before our progress evaluation.
Action: I set up a debugging pipeline, isolated the server connection settings, and found a syntax error in the configuration file. I corrected the script and added connection tests.
Result: The database connected successfully. We passed the evaluation with a 90% score, and my team adopted the connection check template for future projects.

📋Interview Preparation & The 6 C's

I learned that active listening and structured speaking require keeping the **6 C's of Communication** in mind:

  • Clear: I will use plain language and avoid confusing jargon.
  • Concise: I should stick to the point and avoid rambling.
  • Concrete: I will give specific facts and real-life examples.
  • Correct: I must be honest and accurate about my skills.
  • Coherent: I will ensure my points flow logically.
  • Courteous: I will be polite, respectful, and thank the interviewer.

My Preparation Checklist:

  1. 1
    Research: I must research the company's projects, values, and client profile.
  2. 2
    Materials: I will prepare extra CV copies, a notepad, a pen, and my portfolio link.
  3. 3
    Logistics: I will plan my route or check my online software and internet connection beforehand.
  4. 4
    Mock Practice: I will swap roles with peers, record my answers, and check my posture.

My Reflection

I used to be terrified of interviews. I would worry that I would freeze or say something silly. But this session showed me that an interview is just a two-way conversation to see if we are a good fit. That took away so much of my stress! The STAR method also gave me a simple way to answer those tricky "tell me about a time..." questions without rambling.

Doing the mock interview in class was a bit awkward, but it was exactly what I needed. It made me realize that practice is the only way to get better. I'm going to keep practicing my answers, especially using examples from my university projects, so I feel ready when the real interviews start.

Confidence is not about having all the answers. It is about being prepared enough to find them.
