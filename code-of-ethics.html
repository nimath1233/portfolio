Professional Responsibilities & Code of Ethics

Session on computing ethics and professional responsibilities

Before this session I thought ethics was only about following rules. I used to think that as long as you did not break the law, you were doing the right thing. But this session completely changed how I see things.

I learned that ethics is really about making the right choices even when no one is watching. It is about asking yourself — is this fair? Is this honest? Will this hurt someone? Those questions matter a lot more than just ticking boxes on a compliance list.

In IT, our decisions can affect thousands of people. A single piece of code, a database setting, or an algorithm choice can have a huge impact on real lives. That is something I never fully appreciated before this session.

👔Profession, Professional, & Professionalism

I learned that understanding these three foundational concepts is essential as I prepare to enter the corporate and engineering fields:

A Profession

The lecturer explained this as a calling requiring specialized knowledge, academic preparation, and ongoing training that delivers essential services to society (e.g., Software Engineering).

A Professional

I learned that this refers to a person who belongs to a profession, possesses specialized skills, conforms to high standards, and adheres to ethical guidelines.

Professionalism: I realized this refers to the conduct, aims, or qualities that characterize a professional, defined by my competence, integrity, accountability, and commitment to quality.

💻Computing Professionals & Their Social Impact

As an aspiring computing professional, I learned that the systems we build (whether software, databases, or networks) have a deep influence on society, which carries major responsibilities:

  • Critical Systems: I realized that healthcare monitoring, transportation networks, and banking depend directly on the reliability of my software.
  • Daily Life: I learned that communication, education, and daily activities rely heavily on secure and accessible IT applications.
  • High Stakes: The lecturer showed us how a minor coding error or poor security practice on my part can compromise sensitive personal data, lead to massive financial loss, or even cause physical harm.

⚖️What is Ethics & Why Does it Matter?

I learned that Ethics is a set of beliefs about right and wrong behavior that must guide my decision-making, prevent harm, and build public trust.

The session showed me that a Code of Ethics is a formal document outlining guidelines for my professional behavior, defining acceptable conduct when priorities clash.

💡 Why I Need a Code

I realized it acts as a collective conscience for my profession, establishing rules for safety, reliability, and honesty that protect my clients and the public.

⚠️ The Cost of My Neglect

I learned that without ethical guidelines, making profit-driven decisions can compromise user safety, leading to poor code, data leaks, and lost credibility.

🏛️Professional Ethical Frameworks

I learned that computing professionals must act in the interest of multiple stakeholders. The lecturer showed us how major professional bodies outline these duties in their codes of conduct:

Computer Society of Sri Lanka (CSSL)

I learned that CSSL emphasizes my responsibilities to the community, profession, employers, and clients, ensuring IT development benefits the nation.

Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)

The session showed me how ACM focuses on general moral imperatives: contributing to human well-being, avoiding harm, being honest, and respecting privacy.

IEEE Computer Society

I learned that IEEE requires me to commit to making the analysis, design, development, and testing of software a beneficial and respected profession.

British Computer Society (BCS)

I learned that BCS sets standards for public interest, professional competence, duty to relevant authority, and duty to the profession.

Core Professional Responsibilities I Must Uphold:

  1. 1
    To Society: I must ensure my software is safe, respects privacy, is not harmful, and promotes environmental sustainability.
  2. 2
    To Clients/Employers: I must provide honest advice, deliver services competently, respect confidentiality, and avoid conflicts of interest.
  3. 3
    To Colleagues: I must support professional development, respect intellectual property, cooperate on quality checks, and treat others fairly.
  4. 4
    To the Profession: I must maintain technical standards, represent my skills truthfully, and uphold the integrity of the computing community.

🧠Ethical Decision Making & Dilemmas

I realized that ethical dilemmas will occur when there is a conflict between competing duties, such as employer demands vs. public safety, or project timelines vs. rigorous security testing.

An Ethical Decision Framework I Will Use:

  • Identify the Problem: I will gather all facts and identify the stakeholders (users, clients, developers, society) who might be affected.
  • Analyze Options: I must consider alternate courses of action and evaluate their outcomes. I'll ask: Who is helped? Who is harmed?
  • Apply Ethical Codes: I will consult codes like ACM, IEEE, or CSSL to see what standards apply to the situation.
  • Choose Integrity: I will make the decision that minimizes harm, respects human rights, and upholds honesty, and I will document my rationale.
  • Reflect on the Outcome: I want to learn from the consequences and use them to improve my future choices.
💡 Common Dilemmas in Computing:

• Developing systems that collect user data without explicit consent.
• Ignoring known security flaws to meet tight project deadlines.
• Using proprietary libraries in commercial projects without proper licensing.
• Creating biased algorithms that unfairly discriminate against certain groups.

📈Ethical Awareness & Development

Ethical awareness is not a one-time achievement. It is a continuous development process that keeps pace with emerging technologies (like AI, big data, and biotech):

✅ Continuous Growth

✔ Keep updated on ethical issues in new technologies.
✔ Participate in ethics discussions in professional bodies.
✔ Seek feedback on the ethical aspects of your projects.

❌ Pitfalls to Avoid

✘ Assuming compliance with the law is enough.
✘ Believing software developers bear no social responsibility.
✘ Turning a blind eye to unethical practices in your team.

My Reflection

Before this lecture, I thought coding was just about solving puzzles and making things work. I never really thought about how a single bug or a poor security choice could actually hurt someone in real life. Realizing that the systems we build can impact people's privacy or even their safety was a bit scary, but also made me feel proud of what I do.

I don't want to just think of ethics as a list of rules to memorize for an exam. Instead, when I write code or design a database, I want to pause and ask myself if I am keeping the users safe and being honest about my work. It's not about being perfect, it's just about caring enough to do the right thing.

"Technology is neither good nor bad — it is the choices we make while building it that define its impact. Choose with integrity."
