Grooming Etiquette & Personal Branding

Interactive grooming and self-presentation workshop

I honestly did not know what to expect when I walked into this session. The title said “Grooming Etiquettes” and I thought it would just be someone telling us to comb our hair. But it turned out to be one of the most eye-opening sessions I have been to in this whole module. From the moment I sat down, I could feel that this was going to be different.

The facilitator started by talking about professional hygiene — things like keeping your hands clean, making sure your clothes smell fresh, and just being mindful of the people around you in a work setting. Then we moved on to dress codes. I learned that different workplaces have different expectations, and that wearing the right thing is not about fashion — it is about respect. We also spent time on posture, which I never thought of as part of grooming before. Standing tall and making eye contact actually sends a message even before you say a single word.

The part that really stuck with me was the discussion about first impressions. We only get a few seconds to make one, and according to research, people form a judgment about us before we even open our mouths. That was a bit shocking to hear, but it made a lot of sense when I thought about my own experiences. I have definitely judged people by how they looked before getting to know them, and now I understand that others do the same to me.

✨What is Grooming & Etiquette?

I learned that Grooming is the process of caring for my body, maintaining clean habits, and presenting a professional appearance, while Etiquette refers to my code of polite behavior.

First Impressions (7-Second Rule)

The facilitator showed us that it takes only 7 seconds to form a first impression. My posture, dress, and hygiene will speak for me before I say a word.

Personal Branding

I learned that this is the practice of marketing myself as a brand, built through my consistent presentation and reliability.

🧼Daily Hygiene & Personal Grooming

I realized that maintaining high personal hygiene is the absolute foundation of my professional grooming. Distractions can easily undermine my technical competence:

Body & Hand Hygiene

I will bathe daily, wash my hands frequently, and avoid touching my face or hair in meetings.

Oral Care & Breath

I will brush twice daily and carry mints, especially before important meetings.

Hair & Skin Care

I need to keep my hair clean and styled. Facial hair should be neatly trimmed or clean-shaven.

Nails & Fragrance

I will keep my nails clean and short, and use light fragrance to avoid disturbing my coworkers.

The "Grooming Check" I Will Perform: I learned to always look in a mirror before entering an office. I should check if my collar is straight, shoes are clean, and clothes are neat.

👔Dress Code & Appearance Standards

I learned that dressing appropriately displays my respect for the company, clients, and my team. I will ask myself these 5 questions before picking my outfit:

  • 1. Who is my audience? Are they corporate executives (formal) or creative developers (casual)?
  • 2. What is the nature of my event? Is it a daily stand-up, a formal client presentation, or a casual team dinner?
  • 3. Is my clothing comfortable? Can I move, sit, and present easily without constantly adjusting?
  • 4. Are my clothes neat and ironed? I know wrinkles suggest a lack of care and professionalism.
  • 5. Does my style align with company policies? Does it fit the official dress code rules?

Safe Color Coordination:

I was advised to stick to neutral base colors like navy blue or charcoal gray, adding a single accent color for personality. I will avoid overly bright patterns in formal business settings.

🤝Social Etiquette & Respecting Boundaries

I realized that my professional relationships depend on respecting boundaries, diversity, and workspace etiquette:

  1. 1
    Greetings & Handshake: I will greet people warmly with a firm handshake and eye contact to build trust, and repeat names to remember them.
  2. 2
    Respecting Personal Boundaries: I must never touch anyone's hair, hijab, or accessories without permission, nor take photos without consent.
  3. 3
    Accidental Violations & Repair: If I cross a boundary, I will apologize immediately and sincerely without making excuses: "I'm sorry. I didn't realize that made you uncomfortable. I won't repeat it."
  4. 4
    Respect Diversity: I want to learn about and respect diverse cultures, religious practices, and viewpoints in a globalized workplace.
My Reflection

As a student, I'm used to just wearing whatever is comfortable—usually t-shirts and jeans—and not thinking much about it. But this session made me realize that my appearance speaks for me before I even say hello. It was surprising to learn that people form an opinion about us in just 7 seconds! It made me see that dressing neatly is a way of showing respect to others.

In the future, I'm going to be much more careful about how I dress for interviews and presentations. I also noticed that I tend to slouch when I'm tired. I want to work on my posture because sitting and standing tall makes me look and feel much more confident. Small grooming habits really do matter.

Key Takeaway

Your appearance speaks before you do — make sure it is saying something you are proud of.
